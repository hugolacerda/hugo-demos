Glossary of Terms

Common terms you will encounter when selling your business.

EBITDA

Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization. This is a common measure of business profitability used in valuations and deal negotiations. Buyers often express offers as a multiple of EBITDA.

SDE (Seller Discretionary Earnings)

A measure of business cash flow that adds back owner salary and benefits to EBITDA. This is commonly used for smaller, owner-operated businesses where the buyer will take over the owner role.

LOI (Letter of Intent)

A document outlining the basic terms of a proposed transaction, including price, structure, and key conditions. An LOI is typically non-binding except for confidentiality and exclusivity provisions.

Due Diligence

The investigation process a buyer conducts after signing an LOI to verify the information provided about the business. This includes reviewing financials, contracts, legal matters, and operations.

Working Capital

Current assets minus current liabilities. In a business sale, the deal often specifies how much working capital the seller must leave in the business at closing.

Earnout

A portion of the purchase price that is paid after closing based on the business achieving certain performance targets. Earnouts help bridge valuation gaps but add complexity to deals.

Reps and Warranties

Statements by the seller about the condition of the business that become part of the purchase agreement. If these statements turn out to be false, the buyer may have recourse against the seller.

CIM (Confidential Information Memorandum)

A detailed document about the business that is shared with qualified buyers after they sign an NDA. The CIM includes financial history, operations overview, market position, and growth opportunities.
