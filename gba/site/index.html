Common Questions About Selling

We understand the concerns that keep business owners up at night.

How do you protect my confidentiality during the sale?

Confidentiality is built into every step of our process. We use a staged disclosure model where information is released only to pre-qualified buyers who have signed NDAs. Your business name, location, and identifying details are never shared until later stages when a buyer has demonstrated serious intent and financial capability. We never contact your employees, customers, or vendors without your explicit permission.

What happens if my employees find out the business is for sale?

This is one of the most common concerns we hear. Our staged disclosure process is specifically designed to prevent this. Buyer communications happen outside your business hours, site visits are carefully coordinated, and we use blind profiles that describe your business without identifying it. Most of our transactions close without employees ever knowing the business was on the market until the new owner is ready to announce.

How is your valuation different from a broker estimate?

A broker estimate is an informal opinion often based on rules of thumb. Our valuations are prepared by NACVA-certified analysts using accepted methodology that would hold up in court, with the IRS, or in a partner dispute. This gives you a defensible number to anchor negotiations and helps prevent surprises during due diligence.

How long does it take to sell a business?

The average main street business sale takes 8 to 12 months from listing to close. However, this varies based on industry, price point, business quality, and market conditions. Businesses with clean financials, documented processes, and manageable owner involvement tend to sell faster. Our Sellability Score assessment can help you identify factors that might speed up or slow down your timeline.
