Our Confidential Process

How We Protect Your Confidentiality at Every Step

Our staged disclosure process releases information only to qualified buyers who have signed NDAs and demonstrated financial capability. Your business name, location, and identifying details stay protected until you decide a buyer is serious enough to proceed.

The fear of employees, customers, or competitors finding out about a potential sale keeps many business owners from even exploring their options. We designed our process specifically to address this concern.

At each stage, you control what information is shared and with whom. Nothing happens without your explicit approval.

The Staged Disclosure Process

Each stage is designed to protect your confidentiality while moving qualified buyers forward.

1

Discovery Call

A 30-minute confidential conversation to understand your goals, timeline, and business basics. No paperwork, no commitment. This is simply an opportunity for us to learn about your situation and for you to ask questions.

Protected by verbal confidentiality. We do not require any documentation at this stage.
2

Valuation and Preparation

If we move forward, we prepare a formal opinion of value and create your marketing materials. This includes a blind profile that describes your business without identifying it, and a confidential information memorandum for later stages.

Protected by engagement agreement with confidentiality provisions. All materials reviewed and approved by you before use.
3

Blind Marketing

We reach out to potential buyers using your blind profile. This document describes your industry, general location, financial performance, and opportunity without revealing your business name or specific details that could identify you.

No identifying information shared. Buyers see only industry, region, and financial summary.
4

Qualified Introduction

Before any buyer learns your business name, they must sign a non-disclosure agreement and demonstrate financial capability to complete the transaction. Only then do we share the confidential information memorandum and arrange introductions.

NDA signed before identity revealed. Financial qualification verified. You approve each introduction.
5

Due Diligence

Once a letter of intent is signed, the buyer conducts deeper investigation. We coordinate access to sensitive information through a secure data room, manage buyer questions, and help resolve issues that arise.

Information shared through secure data room. Access controlled and logged. NDA enforced throughout.
6

Closing

We coordinate with attorneys, accountants, and lenders to get the deal done. You control the timing and messaging of any announcements to employees, customers, and vendors.

You decide when and how to announce. We help you plan transition communications.

Key Confidentiality Protections

Specific measures we take to keep your sale private.

Blind Profiles

Your business is described by industry, region, and financials without any identifying details. Buyers cannot determine who you are from this document.

NDA Required

Every buyer signs a legally binding non-disclosure agreement before learning your identity. This creates real consequences for breaching confidentiality.

Financial Screening

We verify buyer financial capability before introductions. This prevents tire-kickers and ensures only serious buyers access your information.

Controlled Access

Sensitive documents are shared through secure data rooms with access logging. You know exactly who has seen what and when.

Off-Hours Meetings

Buyer meetings and site visits are scheduled outside business hours or off-site to avoid alerting employees or customers.

Your Control

Nothing happens without your approval. You decide which buyers move forward, what information is shared, and when.

Process Questions

What if a competitor requests information about my business?

We screen all buyer inquiries carefully. If a competitor expresses interest, we discuss it with you before any information is shared. In many cases, you may choose not to proceed with a competitor, or to limit what information they receive. The decision is always yours.

How do you handle site visits without employees noticing?

Site visits are typically scheduled during off-hours, weekends, or times when the business is closed. If that is not possible, we can arrange visits to occur in areas away from employees, or frame the visit as something other than a buyer tour. We work with you to find an approach that protects confidentiality.

What happens if a buyer breaks the NDA?

Our NDAs include specific remedies for breach, including financial damages. While NDA violations are rare in our experience, having a strong agreement in place creates real consequences that discourage careless disclosure. We also limit the amount of sensitive information shared at early stages to reduce risk.

Can I back out if I change my mind?

Yes. Until you sign a purchase agreement, you can decide not to proceed at any time. Many owners start the process and then decide the timing is not right, or that they want to hold onto the business longer. We respect that this is a major decision and that circumstances can change.
