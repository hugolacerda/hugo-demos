Selling Your Business Is a Major Decision

Selling a business is one of the most significant financial decisions you will ever make. The right advisor protects your confidentiality, maximizes your value, and guides you through a process that can take 8 to 12 months from start to finish.

For most business owners, your company represents decades of work and the majority of your net worth. You deserve an advisor who understands the stakes and treats your transaction with the care it requires.

At Gateway Business Advisors, we specialize in helping Florida business owners navigate the sale process with confidence. Our approach is built on three pillars:

1. Confidentiality First

The biggest fear most owners have is that employees, customers, or competitors will find out the business is for sale before the deal closes. Our staged disclosure process is designed specifically to prevent this. We use blind profiles, qualified buyer screening, and carefully coordinated communications to keep your sale confidential until you are ready to announce.

2. Defensible Valuations

A broker estimate based on rules of thumb is not enough when you are negotiating a life-changing transaction. Our valuations are prepared by NACVA-certified analysts using methodology that holds up in court, with the IRS, or in partner disputes. You will know exactly what your business is worth and why.

3. Qualified Buyer Access

Finding a buyer is easy. Finding the right buyer who can close, treat your employees well, and pay a fair price is hard. We maintain relationships with strategic acquirers, private equity groups, and qualified individual buyers across Florida and beyond.

Who We Work With

Gateway works with established, profitable businesses in Florida with the following characteristics:

  • Revenue: $1 million to $20 million in annual sales
  • Profitability: Demonstrable cash flow or adjusted EBITDA
  • History: Typically 5+ years in operation
  • Documentation: Reasonably clean financial records
  • Industries: Manufacturing, distribution, service businesses, healthcare, construction trades, and more

We are selective about the engagements we take because our reputation depends on successful outcomes. If your business is not a fit for our services, we will tell you honestly and point you to resources that can help.

What to Expect When You Work With Us

The business sale process typically takes 8 to 12 months from engagement to close. Here is a high-level overview:

  1. Discovery Call: A confidential 30-minute conversation about your goals, timeline, and business basics.
  2. Valuation: A formal opinion of value from our NACVA-certified team.
  3. Marketing Preparation: We create a blind profile and confidential information memorandum.
  4. Buyer Outreach: Targeted outreach to qualified buyers in our network.
  5. Negotiation: We manage offers, counteroffers, and deal structure.
  6. Due Diligence: Supporting you through buyer investigation.
  7. Closing: Coordinating with attorneys and accountants to get the deal done.

Is Now the Right Time to Sell?

Timing a business sale involves both market factors and personal readiness. Consider these questions:

  • Is your business growing, stable, or declining? Buyers pay more for growth.
  • Are your financials clean and documented? Banks need clear records to finance acquisitions.
  • How dependent is the business on you personally? High owner dependence reduces value.
  • What is your post-sale plan? Buyers want to know you have thought this through.
  • Are there industry trends that favor selling now versus later?

If you are not sure, our Sellability Score assessment can help you evaluate your readiness and identify areas to improve before going to market.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are your fees?

Our fee structure is typical for the industry: a success fee calculated as a percentage of the final sale price, paid at closing. For most main street transactions, this ranges from 10-12%. Lower middle market deals may have different structures. We are happy to discuss specifics during our initial conversation.

Do I need to sign an exclusive agreement?

Yes, we work on an exclusive basis. This allows us to invest fully in marketing your business, coordinate buyer communications, and protect confidentiality. Our engagement agreements typically run 12 months, which reflects the realistic timeline for most transactions.

Can I still run my business during the sale process?

Absolutely, and you should. Buyers want to acquire a business that is performing well. We structure the process to minimize disruption to your operations. Most communication happens outside business hours, and we handle buyer inquiries so you can focus on running your company.

What if I am not ready to sell yet?

That is perfectly fine. Many owners start the conversation 2-3 years before they plan to exit. This gives you time to address any issues that might reduce value, improve documentation, and plan your transition. Our Sellability Score assessment is a great starting point for owners who are planning ahead.
